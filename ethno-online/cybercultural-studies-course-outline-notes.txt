Week 3 Perspectives on Cyberculture: Anthropology and the Internet - Cris Shore

Modern information and communication technologies (ICTs) provide an extremely useful tool for gathering and analysing anthropological data. However, ICTs - particularly the Internet - have also had a major impact on the shape and character of contemporary culture, particularly in the way people work, think and relate to the world (and to one another). In short, the revolution in information technology is itself a fascinating anthropological phenomenon. This session explores some of the effects of IT on our contemporary world. It also looks at the implications for anthropology and probes what an 'Anthropology of the Internet' might entail.

Questions to consider
    * What are the implications of the rise of the Internet for anthropological analysis?
    * What impact has the Internet had on the fabric of contemporary society? In what ways are the cultural consequences f the Internet comparable to other forms of IT?
    * Is the increasing use of computer technology in third world countries (for example, Egypt) evidence of 'Westernization'?
